What is a scholarship? −

A scholarship is a financial award for a student to further their education and is not required to be repaid.These scholarships are intended to be added to other financial supports you receive.

I don’t even know how to start looking for scholarships. Where do I start? +

You can begin by visiting scholarship portals, reaching out to educational institutions, or consulting with a scholarship advisor to help find options that fit your qualifications.

How many scholarships can I apply for? +

There’s no limit to how many scholarships you can apply for. It’s often recommended to apply for as many as you qualify for to increase your chances of receiving financial aid.

How do I receive my scholarship funds? +

Scholarship funds are typically disbursed directly to your school to cover tuition and fees. In some cases, you may receive a check or electronic transfer for other expenses.

Do I have to fill out a FAFSA to get a scholarship? +

FAFSA is required for some scholarships, especially those offered by government programs or institutions that take financial need into consideration. However, not all scholarships require it.
