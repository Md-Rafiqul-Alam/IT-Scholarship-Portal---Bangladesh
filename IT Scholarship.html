Islamic Development Bank (IsDB) Scholarships

The IsDB provides scholarships for students in various fields, including IT. They focus on supporting students from member countries, including Bangladesh, to pursue undergraduate, master's, Ph.D., and vocational training programs.


Citizenship:

Applicants must be citizens of Bangladesh or any other IsDB member country.

Field of Study:

The scholarship supports studies in information technology (IT), computer science, software engineering, and related fields in technology, science, and engineering.

Other priority fields include agriculture, medicine, pharmacy, economics, and development studies, but IT is a core focus for those looking to develop technical skills.

Educational Qualification:

For Undergraduate Programs:

Applicants should have completed Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) or equivalent, with excellent academic records (generally a minimum GPA of 4.5 out of 5).

For Master’s or PhD Programs:

Applicants must have completed a relevant Bachelor’s degree (for Master’s) or Master’s degree (for PhD) in IT-related fields with a strong academic record.

The scholarship also covers post-doctoral research in IT and technology fields.

Age Limits:

For Undergraduate: Applicants must be between 18 and 24 years old.

For Master’s: Applicants must be under 35 years old.

For PhD: Applicants must be under 40 years old.

For Post-doctoral research: Applicants must be below 45 years.

Academic Performance:

Applicants must demonstrate strong academic performance, especially in mathematics, science, and technology subjects.

A minimum GPA requirement is often expected for both secondary and higher education levels (typically a GPA of 3.5 or higher on a 4.0 scale for Master’s/PhD candidates).

Financial Need:

The IsDB Scholarship is designed to support students from financially disadvantaged backgrounds. Applicants must demonstrate financial need and an inability to pursue higher education without the scholarship.

Commitment to Development:

The scholarship prioritizes applicants who demonstrate a commitment to the socio-economic development of Bangladesh and are willing to return to their home country after completing their studies to contribute to national development.

Personal Development and Leadership Skills:

Candidates with demonstrated leadership abilities, community involvement, and a proactive approach to solving social or technological issues are often given preference.

ICT Division, Government of Bangladesh

The ICT Division offers various scholarships and training programs to students pursuing IT-related degrees.


Eligibility Criteria :

The applicant must be a citizen of Bangladesh.

Academic Qualifications :

The applicant should be a student of a recognized educational institution in Bangladesh.Generally, applicants should be pursuing or willing to pursue studies in ICT-related fields, such as computer science, software engineering, information technology, etc. A minimum academic performance, often around a CGPA of 3.00 (on a 4.00 scale) or equivalent marks, is typically required. However, this may vary based on the specific scholarship.

Age Limit:

Some scholarships may have an upper age limit, typically around 18-30 years for most programs, but this can vary depending on the specific scheme.

Financial Need :

Some scholarships prioritize students from financially disadvantaged backgrounds.

Proof of financial hardship, such as family income statements, might be required.

Gender Inclusivity:

Special provisions or additional points may be given to female students to promote gender balance in ICT fields.

Other Requirements :

Participation in specific national or government initiatives related to ICT, like coding competitions or innovation programs, may be considered an advantage. Applicants may need to be involved in ICT-related projects or training programs.

Citizenship:

Applicants must be citizens of Bangladesh.

Education Level:

For most scholarships, applicants are expected to have completed at least a secondary or higher secondary education (SSC or HSC).

Some scholarships are targeted at undergraduate or graduate-level students in IT-related fields.

Field of Study:

The applicant should be studying or planning to study in IT or a related field (such as computer science, software engineering, information systems, etc.).

Age Limit:

There may be an age limit for certain scholarships, typically under 30 or 35 years old.

Academic Performance:

A good academic record is often required. This might mean having a minimum GPA in previous examinations.

Scholarships may also require strong performance in specific subjects like mathematics and science.

Skill Level:

Financial Need:

Some scholarships may prioritize students from low-income families or underprivileged backgrounds.

Examinations/Interviews:

In some cases, applicants may have to pass an entrance exam or interview to demonstrate their qualifications.

Depending on the type of scholarship, specific skill certifications (like IT certifications) or relevant work experience may be needed.

Bdjobs IT Scholarships

Bdjobs offers scholarships for IT courses, focusing on skill development through their training portal.

Citizenship:

Applicants must be citizens of Bangladesh.

Educational Qualification:

Applicants typically need to have at least completed Higher Secondary Certificate (HSC) or equivalent.

For certain advanced courses, undergraduate or graduate-level students in IT or related fields may be preferred.

Interest in IT:

Applicants should have a strong interest in learning IT skills or pursuing a career in IT.

Some basic knowledge in computers or programming may be required depending on the type of course or scholarship.

Age Limit:

There may be an age limit for certain programs, generally targeting young professionals or students in the early stages of their careers (under 30-35 years old).

Academic Performance:

A strong academic background is often considered, but the main focus is usually on the applicant’s passion and potential in IT.

Financial Need:

Scholarships may give preference to candidates from financially disadvantaged backgrounds, helping those who may not have the resources to pay for IT courses otherwise.

Skill Level:

Depending on the scholarship program, basic computer literacy or familiarity with software may be required.

For more advanced IT courses (e.g., coding, networking, etc.), applicants may need prior knowledge or experience.

Examinations/Interviews:

Some scholarships might require applicants to sit for a selection test or an interview to assess their aptitude or motivation for the course.

Commitment to Career Development:

Applicants must show a genuine interest in developing their IT skills for future employment or entrepreneurial ventures in the field of technology.

Robi Axiata

Robi provides scholarships for IT students as part of their CSR initiatives, supporting education in tech fields.

Citizenship:

Applicants must be citizens of Bangladesh.

Educational Qualification:

Typically aimed at undergraduate or graduate-level students.

Applicants should be pursuing a degree in IT-related fields such as Computer Science, Software Engineering, Information Technology, or a similar discipline.

Academic Performance:

Applicants need to have a strong academic background.

A minimum GPA requirement (often 3.0 or higher on a 4.0 scale) in their previous examinations (SSC, HSC, or University exams) is common.

Age Limit:

Generally aimed at young students, so applicants are often required to be below a certain age, typically under 30 years old.

Skills and Competence:

A demonstrated interest in IT, technology, coding, or digital innovation.

Previous knowledge or certifications in programming languages, networking, or IT infrastructure may be beneficial but not mandatory.

Financial Need:

Some scholarships might consider applicants from economically disadvantaged backgrounds to prioritize those who may not have the financial means to support their education.

Involvement in Extracurricular Activities:

Active participation in extracurricular activities, particularly those related to technology, coding competitions, or hackathons, could be an added advantage.

Innovation and Creativity:

Some scholarship programs under Robi Axiata might prioritize applicants who demonstrate innovative ideas in technology or have participated in projects that showcase their IT or digital skills.

Community Impact:

Applicants who demonstrate leadership, social responsibility, or have contributed to their communities through IT projects or initiatives may be given preference.
